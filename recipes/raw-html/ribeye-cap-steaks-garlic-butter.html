Ribeye Cap Steaks with Garlic Butter

⏱ 20 min 🍽 2 servings 🍷 David & Anne Only
740Calories
54gProtein
56gFat
2gNet Carbs

Ribeye cap (spinalis dorsi) — the most prized cut on the cow — seared hard in cast iron and basted with garlic butter until deeply crusty. Served with roasted mushrooms and a wedge salad.

Ingredients
Steaks
·2 ribeye cap steaks (or regular ribeyes)
·3 tbsp butter
·4 cloves garlic, smashed
·4 sprigs fresh thyme
·1 tbsp avocado oil
·Coarse salt and black pepper
Mushrooms
·16 oz mixed mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, oyster)
·2 tbsp butter
·3 cloves garlic, sliced
·Fresh thyme
·Salt and pepper
Wedge Salad
·1 head iceberg, quartered
·Blue cheese dressing
·1/2 cup blue cheese crumbles
·4 strips bacon, cooked and crumbled
·Cherry tomatoes
Instructions
1
Rest and Season Steak
Pull steaks from fridge 20 minutes before cooking. Pat completely dry. Season aggressively with coarse salt and pepper on all sides.
2
Roast Mushrooms
Toss mushrooms with butter, garlic slices, thyme, salt, and pepper. Roast at 400°F for 20 minutes while you cook the steak.
3
Cook Bacon
Cook bacon until crispy for the wedge salad.
4
Sear Steak
Heat cast iron over highest heat until smoking. Add avocado oil. Place steaks and don't move for 3-4 minutes until deeply browned. Flip.
5
Butter Baste
Add butter, smashed garlic, and thyme sprigs. Tilt pan and continuously spoon foaming butter over steaks for 2 minutes. Internal temp 130°F = medium-rare.
6
Rest and Plate
Rest 5 minutes on a wire rack. Serve with mushrooms and wedge salad with blue cheese, bacon, and tomatoes.

David's Notes

→ Ribeye cap is the spinalis — if your butcher doesn't carry it, ask them to cut it. Worth seeking out.
→ The butter basting step is what separates a good steak from a great steak.
→ Finish strong — Week 6, Thursday, you've made it almost to the end. Celebrate with the best steak in the rotation.
