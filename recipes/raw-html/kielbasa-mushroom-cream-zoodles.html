Kielbasa & Mushroom Cream Sauce over Zoodles

⏱ 25 min 🍽 4 servings 👦 Teddy Approved
440Calories
22gProtein
36gFat
8gNet Carbs

Sliced kielbasa sautéed with mushrooms in a silky garlic cream sauce, served over spiralized zucchini noodles. Restaurant-quality comfort food in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients
Main
·1.5 lbs kielbasa, sliced into rounds
·16 oz cremini mushrooms, sliced
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1 cup heavy cream
·1/2 cup chicken broth
·1/2 cup grated parmesan
·2 tbsp butter
·1 tbsp olive oil
·1 tsp thyme
·Salt and pepper
·Fresh parsley
Base
·4 medium zucchini, spiralized (or 2 bags pre-spiralized)
·1 tbsp olive oil
·Salt
Salad
·4 cups arugula
·3 tbsp olive oil
·2 tbsp lemon juice
·Salt and pepper
·Shaved parmesan
Instructions
1
Prep Zoodles
Toss spiralized zucchini with olive oil and salt. Set aside — they'll release water and soften slightly on their own without cooking.
2
Sear Kielbasa
Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Sear kielbasa until browned on both sides, about 4 minutes. Remove and set aside.
3
Cook Mushrooms
In the same pan, add butter. Add mushrooms and cook without stirring for 3-4 minutes until golden. Season with salt and thyme.
4
Add Garlic and Cream
Add garlic, cook 30 seconds. Pour in chicken broth and reduce by half — about 2 minutes. Add heavy cream and bring to a gentle simmer. Cook 3-4 minutes until slightly thickened.
5
Finish Sauce
Stir in parmesan until melted. Return kielbasa to pan and coat in sauce.
6
Plate
Place zoodles in bowls. Top with kielbasa and mushroom cream sauce. Garnish with parsley and serve with arugula salad.

David's Notes

→ Don't cook the zoodles — the sauce heats them perfectly and they stay al dente.
→ Add a splash of white wine to the sauce after the broth for extra depth if you have it open.
→ This is legitimately impressive for a weeknight dinner.
