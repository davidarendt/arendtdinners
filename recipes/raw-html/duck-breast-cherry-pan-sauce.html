Duck Breast with Cherry Pan Sauce

⏱ 30 min 🍽 2 servings 🍷 David & Anne Only
580Calories
44gProtein
38gFat
9gNet Carbs

Duck breast scored and seared skin-side down until shatteringly crispy, finished in the oven, and served with a cherry balsamic pan sauce. Elegant, impressive, and surprisingly simple.

Ingredients
Duck
·2 duck breasts
·Salt and black pepper
·Fresh thyme
Cherry Pan Sauce
·1 cup fresh or frozen cherries, pitted
·1/2 cup beef or chicken stock
·2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
·1 tbsp butter
·1 shallot, minced
·Salt and pepper
Sides
·1 lb green beans, trimmed
·2 tbsp butter
·Salt and pepper
·4 cups mixed greens
·2 tbsp shallot vinaigrette
Instructions
1
Score and Season
Score duck skin in a crosshatch pattern without cutting into the meat. Season generously with salt and pepper. Let sit at room temperature 15 minutes.
2
Preheat Oven
Preheat oven to 400°F.
3
Render the Skin
Place duck breasts skin-side DOWN in a cold oven-safe skillet. Turn heat to medium and cook 12-15 minutes without moving, pouring off rendered fat periodically, until skin is deep golden and crispy.
4
Finish in Oven
Flip duck breasts. Transfer skillet to oven and cook 6-8 minutes for medium (pink inside). Rest 5 minutes.
5
Make Pan Sauce
Pour off most of the duck fat (save it — it's liquid gold for cooking). Cook shallot in remaining fat 1 minute. Add cherries, stock, and balsamic. Simmer 5 minutes until slightly thickened. Swirl in butter, season.
6
Cook Green Beans
Sauté green beans in butter with salt and pepper until tender-crisp.
7
Plate
Slice duck breast diagonally. Fan over plate with green beans, spoon cherry sauce over top. Serve with dressed greens.

David's Notes

→ Starting skin-side down in a cold pan is the correct technique — it renders the fat slowly.
→ Save the duck fat in a jar in the fridge — it makes incredible roasted vegetables.
→ This is as impressive as dinner gets. Save it for when you want to feel like you're at a restaurant.
