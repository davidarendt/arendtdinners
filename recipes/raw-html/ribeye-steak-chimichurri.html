Ribeye Steak & Chimichurri

⏱ 25 min 🍽 2 servings 🍷 David & Anne Only
680Calories
48gProtein
52gFat
4gNet Carbs

Pan-seared ribeye basted in butter with a bright, herbaceous chimichurri sauce and roasted asparagus. A date-night level dinner on a weeknight timeline.

Ingredients
Steak
·2 ribeye steaks (1 inch thick)
·2 tbsp butter
·3 cloves garlic, smashed
·2 sprigs fresh rosemary
·Salt and coarse black pepper
·1 tbsp neutral oil (avocado or grapeseed)
Chimichurri
·1 cup fresh parsley, packed
·4 cloves garlic
·1/3 cup olive oil
·2 tbsp red wine vinegar
·1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
·1/2 tsp salt
·1/4 tsp black pepper
Sides
·1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
·2 tbsp olive oil
·Salt and pepper
·2 cups arugula
·Shaved parmesan
·Lemon wedges
Instructions
1
Make Chimichurri
Pulse parsley, garlic, olive oil, red wine vinegar, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper in a food processor or finely chop by hand. Set aside at room temp — sauce improves as it sits.
2
Roast Asparagus
Toss asparagus with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast at 425°F for 12-15 minutes until tender with slight char.
3
Sear the Steak
Pat steaks completely dry. Season generously with salt and pepper on both sides. Heat oil in a cast iron skillet over high heat until smoking. Sear steaks 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare.
4
Butter Baste
In the last 2 minutes, add butter, garlic, and rosemary to the pan. Tilt pan and spoon foaming butter over steaks repeatedly.
5
Rest
Transfer steaks to a cutting board and rest 5 minutes before slicing. This is non-negotiable — it keeps juices in the meat.
6
Plate
Plate steak with asparagus, chimichurri spooned over top, and arugula with shaved parmesan and lemon on the side.

David's Notes

→ Dry steak = better sear. Pat completely dry with paper towels before seasoning.
→ Chimichurri keeps a week in the fridge and is incredible on eggs, chicken, or any protein.
→ This is your David-and-Anne night — Teddy gets nuggets and you get the good stuff.
