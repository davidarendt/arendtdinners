Rack of Lamb with Herb Crust

⏱ 30 min 🍽 2 servings 🍷 David & Anne Only
620Calories
42gProtein
46gFat
4gNet Carbs

Frenched rack of lamb coated in Dijon, garlic, rosemary, and parsley crust, roasted to a perfect pink medium-rare. One of the most impressive meals you can cook in 30 minutes.

Ingredients
Lamb
·1 frenched rack of lamb (8 bones, about 1.5 lbs)
·2 tbsp Dijon mustard
·3 cloves garlic, minced
·2 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
·2 tbsp fresh parsley, minced
·2 tbsp olive oil
·Salt and coarse black pepper
Sides
·1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
·2 tbsp olive oil
·Salt and pepper
·4 cups arugula
·Lemon vinaigrette
·Shaved parmesan
Instructions
1
Preheat and Prep
Preheat oven to 400°F. Let lamb sit at room temperature 15 minutes. Season all sides generously with salt and pepper.
2
Make Crust
Mix together Dijon mustard, garlic, rosemary, parsley, and olive oil.
3
Sear
Heat an oven-safe skillet over high heat. Sear lamb fat-side down for 3-4 minutes until golden. Flip and sear other sides briefly.
4
Apply Crust
Remove from heat. Brush meat side generously with Dijon herb mixture.
5
Roast
Roast at 400°F for 12-14 minutes for medium-rare (internal temp 130°F). Rest 5 minutes.
6
Roast Asparagus
Toss asparagus with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast alongside lamb for 12 minutes.
7
Serve
Cut between bones into individual chops. Fan on plate with asparagus and arugula salad with lemon vinaigrette.

David's Notes

→ Rack of lamb is expensive but worth it for a Saturday dinner — shops for it at Costco if you have one nearby.
→ A meat thermometer is your best friend here — 130°F internal is perfect medium-rare.
→ Letting it rest is crucial — the juices redistribute and it finishes cooking to a perfect 135°F.
