Greek Ground Beef Skillet

⏱ 25 min 🍽 4 servings 👦 Teddy Approved
420Calories
38gProtein
28gFat
8gNet Carbs

Bold Mediterranean flavors in one pan — ground beef seared with garlic and oregano, finished with wilted spinach, blistered cherry tomatoes, kalamata olives, and crumbled feta over cauliflower rice.

Ingredients
Main Skillet
·1.5 lbs ground beef (80/20)
·3 cups fresh spinach
·1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
·1/2 cup kalamata olives, halved
·4 oz crumbled feta cheese
·4 cloves garlic, minced
·1 tsp dried oregano
·1/2 tsp cumin
·1/2 tsp smoked paprika
·2 tbsp olive oil
·1 lemon, juiced
·Salt and black pepper
·Fresh parsley to garnish
Base
·1 bag (12 oz) frozen cauliflower rice
Tzatziki Side
·1/2 cup full-fat Greek yogurt
·1 cup cucumber, finely diced
·1 clove garlic, minced
·Pinch of salt and fresh dill
Instructions
1
Cook Cauliflower Rice
Cook frozen cauliflower rice per package directions. Season with salt and a drizzle of olive oil. Set aside and keep warm.
2
Brown the Beef
Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Add ground beef and break apart as it cooks. Season with oregano, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook until browned, about 7-8 minutes. Drain excess fat if needed.
3
Bloom the Garlic
Push beef to one side. Add remaining olive oil and garlic to the empty space. Cook 30 seconds until fragrant, then stir together with the beef.
4
Add Tomatoes & Olives
Add cherry tomatoes and kalamata olives. Stir and cook 2-3 minutes until tomatoes just begin to blister.
5
Wilt the Spinach
Add spinach in handfuls, stirring until wilted — about 2 minutes. Squeeze half the lemon over the top and stir to combine.
6
Plate & Finish
Serve beef mixture over cauliflower rice. Top with crumbled feta, remaining lemon juice, and fresh parsley. Serve tzatziki alongside.

David's Notes

→ 80/20 beef gives you the fat you need on keto — don't drain it all, just excess.
→ Teddy-approved: the ground texture and mild spice profile work well for kids.
→ Make extra tzatziki — it keeps 3 days and works great as a dip all week.
→ Leftovers reheat perfectly — better the next day once flavors marry.
